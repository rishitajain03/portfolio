About Me

My journey began with a fascination for biology and how living systems work. During my undergraduate studies in Biotechnology, I gained hands-on laboratory experience that helped me understand the experimental side of research.

As I explored bioinformatics and transcriptomics, I discovered how computational approaches could unlock insights hidden within biological data. Working with RNA-seq datasets at CSIR-IGIB strengthened my interest in combining biology, data analysis, and artificial intelligence to solve meaningful healthcare challenges.

Today, my goal is to contribute to technologies that improve precision medicine by applying computational methods to complex biological problems.
